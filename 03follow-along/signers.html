Who were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most well-known include:

Thomas Jefferson
The principal author of the Declaration of Independence, Jefferson is probably the most famous signer. He later became the third President of the United States.
John Adams
A key figure in the American Revolution and a driving force behind the push for independence, Adams was a delegate from Massachusetts. He later became the second President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
Known for his diplomacy, wit, and contributions to science, Franklin was a respected elder statesman at the time of the signing. He was also a key negotiator during the Treaty of Paris that ended the Revolutionary War.
Samuel Adams
A cousin of John Adams, Samuel Adams was a passionate patriot and a leader in the movement for independence, known for his role in organizing protests like the Boston Tea Party.
John Hancock
Famous for his large, bold signature on the Declaration, Hancock was a prominent merchant and a president of the Continental Congress. He was also a key financial supporter of the revolutionary cause.
Roger Sherman
A founding father from Connecticut, Sherman was the only person to sign all four major American documents: the Continental Association, the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, and the U.S. Constitution.
Francis Lewis
A signer from New York, Lewis was a wealthy merchant who suffered personal losses due to the war, including the destruction of his property and the capture of his wife by the British.

These men were central to the movement for independence and went on to shape the future of the United States in various ways.
